Hello! Welcome to my webpage. I am currently a Postdoctoral researcher at the department of Biostatistics & Informatics, School of Public Health, University of Colorado, Anschutz Medical Campus. My research includes medical image computing and imaging informatics, Bayesian nonparametric modeling, mHealth & Digital Health. I work cohesively with my supervisor Prof. Debashis Ghosh, Prof. Elena Hsieh, Prof. Fuyong Xing and Prof. Susan Moore. I received my Ph.D. in EECS from University of California, Irvine and focused on social and biophysical network analysis, and pervasive computing.

Research Interests

My research interest involves a broad range of topics: development and application of computational and statistical techniques to theoretical and methodological problems within the areas of computational biology, imaging analysis, social and biophysical network analysis, and pervasive computing. Currently, my work focuses on: prediction and classification of phenotypical cellular biomarkers of tumor cells; Bayesian methods for clustering, inference and properties of large-scale, high-dimensional cytometry datasets; time series and frequency analysis for cohort-based mobile health solutions; In the past, I worked on the structure of spatially embedded large-scale interpersonal and telecommunication networks; spectural analysis and applications for cross-regional and dynamic communication data.
